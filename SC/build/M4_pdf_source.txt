The various steps involved in designing a fuzzy logic controller are as follows: i
| Step 1: Locate the input, ourput and state variables of the plane under consideration. }
Step 2: Split che complece universe of discourse spanned by each variable into a number of fuzzy subsets,
assigning each with a linguistic label. The subsets include all che elements in the universe. .
Step 3: Obtain the membership function for each fuzzy subset.
Step 4: Assign the fuzzy relationships between the inputs or states of fuzzy subsets on one side and the
oucpucs of fuzzy subsets on other side, thereby forming the rule base. 7
Step 5: Choose appropriate scaling factors for the input and ourput variables for normalizing the variables
berween (0, 1] and [—1, 1) interval.
Step 6: Carry out the fuzification process.
Step 7: Identify the ouput contributed from cach rule using fuzzy approximate reasoning.
Step 8: Combine che fuzzy outputs obtained from each rule. i
| Step 9: Finally, apply defuzzification to form a crisp output. | t
The above steps are performed and executed for a simple FLC system. The following design elements are
adopted for designing a general FLC system: t
1, Fuzzification strategies and che interpretation of a fuzzifier.
2. Fuzzy knowledge base: t
normalization of the parameters involved; i
partitioning of input and output spaces; :
selection of membership functions of a primary fuzzy set. t
3. Fuzzy rule base: :
selection of input and output variables; H
source from which fuzzy control rules are to be derived; ‘
types of fuzzy control rules; H
compleceness of fuzzy control rules. }
4. Decision-making logic: ;
proper definition of fuzzy implication;
interpretation of connective “and”;
interpretation of connective “or”;
inference engine, |
5. Defuzzification strategies and the interpretation of a defuzzifier. ;
